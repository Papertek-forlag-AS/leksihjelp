<decisions_made>

- **Pre-gzip bundle (sibling repo) over splitting de in two:** when de bundle was 4.49 MiB right under Vercel's 4.5 MB cap, chose pre-gzip (5.8× compression, drops to ~800 KB, gives every language headroom) over splitting the API into core+extras (would have invasively complicated client cache, atomic swap, and revision tracking forever). Logged as Task 4 follow-up to 23-01; deferred-items.md cleared.
- **Live-patch the four regressions in this session rather than spawning a new executor:** all four were small, isolated, and the user was actively testing. Waiting on a separate plan/executor cycle would have lost the debugging momentum. Each fix has its own commit so the audit trail stays clean.
- **Synthesize `_metadata` in vocab-store rather than migrate popup.js call sites:** popup.js has dozens of `dictionary._metadata.*` references; adding a 4-line shim in the cache adapter is much smaller surface area than migrating every call site, and the bundle's top-level `language`/`languageName` make synthesis trivial.
- **Tolerated the `Accept-Encoding: identity` quirk** (server always returns gzip even when client requests identity) as a known harmless deviation. Documented in 23-01-SUMMARY. Real browsers always send gzip; only naive curl scripts are affected and they get correct decoded bytes via `--compressed`.

</decisions_made>
